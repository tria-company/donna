### A Money Model Happened

**A Money Model is a sequence of offers.** At their core, we find every opportunity to solve a customer's problem...and then offer to solve it. For that reason, Money Models tend to have many offers in a specific order. If you offer the right thing when customers realize they need it, you can make as many offers as you like.

This is the rental car company's Money Model stated plainly:

- **Offer #1:** Vehicle Upgrade
- **Offer #2:** Late Return
- **Offer #3:** Premium Insurance
- **Offer #4:** Minimum Insurance Downsell
- **Offer #5:** Prepaid Gas

So yeah, I paid more, but it also solved more problems. Let's break down the problems she solved:

- She solved my "big man in a small car" problem by offering a vehicle that had more space.
- She solved my "late checkout" problem by offering the flexibility to keep the vehicle longer.
- She solved my "worries about dinging the car" by offering insurance to protect against it.
- She solved my "risk of missing my flight" problem by offering a way to prepay for the gas ahead of time so I wouldn't have to do it on my ride back.

...And all those things cost money I was happy to pay.

The rental car company thought out every nuance. They told me about the problem, then made their solution available to me. They offered solutions for higher fees and hassles I might have had later for smaller fees in total right now.

As a result, my $19 rental became a $100 rental. I paid more money faster. And now, we can see why the car rental industry brings in billions in the United States alone...per month. A successful Money Model.

### Beware: Bad Money Models Kill Businesses

It costs many businesses more to get somebody to buy a thing than they make in profit off the thing. In other words, they lose money getting new customers — that's a big problem.

And here's what happens...

- They spend money to get customers.
- At the end of the month, they realize they spent more than they made.
- They cut back on advertising.
- Get fewer customers than they can handle because they can't afford them.
- Then, cut advertising altogether.
- Float the business with personal cash, loans, credit, and then...pray for profit.
- Sell percentages of their business just to keep the lights on.
- Wait months (or years!) to make their money back...if ever.
- Fall further and further behind until...
- Finally, they lose it all.

But it doesn't have to be that way. There's plenty of money. You just have to go get it.

In traditional business, the slow drip of profits from lots of customers eventually pays for a single customer. This "drip" starves the business of cash. It means they can only get lots of customers through advertising...if they already have lots of customers! Big companies (or small companies with investors) can do this because they have the money to burn.

Think about it this way. If you spend $100 in advertising to get a customer and make $500 in profit from them, that's a great deal. You should take it all day. But what if it takes you two years to make your cash back? It's a great business...if you already have tons of cash in the bank. Otherwise, you're gonna run out of money. That leaves you with two options:

- **Option #1:** Wait two years to get paid and pray you don't run out of money.
- **Option #2:** Get paid fast and grow as much as you darn well please.

**A good Money Model is option 2.**

### Four Types of Offers

There are four types of offers: **Attraction Offers, Upsell Offers, Downsell Offers, and Continuity Offers.** All improve our Money Model, but they all do it differently. They work great on their own, but together they make your business unstoppable.

1. **Attraction Offers** turn strangers into customers.
2. **Upsell Offers** get people to spend more cash.
3. **Downsell Offers** get people to say yes when they would have said no.
4. **Continuity Offers** keep people buying.

If you look at great businesses, you'll see different versions of these offers as core components of their money-making engine. You can use one, two, multiples of one, or all four together. You can combine them however you want. But, when I look at my most profitable businesses, I used all four. And here's why:

If you don't have an offer for getting customers, you won't get as many. But let's say you do. If you only have that one thing to offer, you won't make nearly as much money as you could. So if you have something to offer next, an upsell, you'll finally get some cash. But, you still won't make as much as you could because lots of people will still say "no." So, we turn those "nos" into "yeses" with downsells. And that works fine. But it would be even better if you had that extra cash guaranteed to come in month after month. So, you make a continuity offer to top it off. That's how I like to do it.

### Win Your Money Back

**If you do X within Y time within Z rules, you can get it free.**

**June 2013.**

I was in a room full of experienced gym owners, and I was the new guy. We all took turns talking about what was working well. That's when Danny piped up.

"Yea...so as you guys know, I've been struggling with sales...and I think I got it figured out. I had this pain in the butt guy who wouldn't buy anything. He knew he needed it but he also said he needed more accountability. So we were going back and forth, and finally he came up with this idea. He said, 'How about this: I give you $500. You train me for eight weeks. And if I hit my goal, I get my money back. But in return, you can use my results to market your business. Fair enough?'"

"So...what happened?" I asked.

Danny replied, "I figured he wasn't gonna buy anyways, so I sold him."

"Okay, so what happened with the guy?"

"He hit the goal."

"So did you give him his money back?"

"That's what you'd think, but he ended up using the money to buy more training!"

"Seems decent enough. What about marketing his results?"

"Dude, marketing his before and after pictures brought in thirteen referrals!"

"That's insane. Now we're talking."

"Yea, I know. I offer this to everyone now. The results are way better and people love the offer. And all the free advertising they do for us gets their friends and family to join too. I'm making more money than ever."

***

This is the first time I ever saw an offer like this. I updated it over time, but the core stayed the same: **pay now with a chance to get your money back later.** I used it for private training, group training, private nutrition coaching, and group nutrition coaching. Once I saw how well it worked with my current customers, I started putting the offer in my ads for new customers. My cost of getting customers went way down and my leads exploded!

#### Description

A Win Your Money Back Offer works like this. You set a goal for the customer and tell them how to reach it. If they reach it, then they qualify to get their money back or get it back as store credit.

This offer grew my gyms better than any other. It was also the first Grand Slam Offer that Gym Launch taught to gym owners. It has tons of flexibility. So if you want to get more cash, get more customers, and get them better results. Nothing beats it.

To "Win Your Money Back" the person has three options: **Get Results, Take Actions, or both.** And to make this work, you have to make the results and actions simple to track.

**Results:** Here, no matter what they do, if the customer gets the result, they win their money back. For example: Making $X a month, Getting Y customers, Losing Z lbs etc. Basically, they bet on their own ability to reach the goal.

**Actions:** Here, you hold them accountable for doing actions instead of getting results. No matter what results they get, if the customer does what you ask, they win their money back. For example: attend all sessions, calls, meetings, log progress, take pictures, do assigned homework, etc. Here, they bet on their ability to follow directions.

**Actions and Results:** Here, you hold customers accountable to following directions and getting results. If they do both, they win their money back. Often, people wanting to achieve a goal have too few skills to do it. Even if they did bet on themselves, they'd fail. By setting a good goal for them and showing how they reach it, they have a fighting chance. Here they bet on their ability to follow directions and that your directions will get them the result.

**Bottom Line:** Customers put money down. If they do the stuff OR they get the result OR both — they get it back as cash or store credit.

- Take pictures and be in a press release.

(This was an actual offer.)

#### Important Notes

**This offer has generated over $1 billion in sales industry-wide. It works.**

**Win Your Money Back Works With New, Current, And Previous Customers.** I like to use it with new customers because it offers the steepest discount possible — 100%. I like it with current customers because it mixes them in with new customers. And I like to use it to get previous customers back because bigger incentives get them to come back.

**It Works Well With Stuff People Start And...Quit.** Like starting businesses, learning new skills, losing weight, building fitness, beauty regimes, self-care, time management, mental health management, etc. It keeps motivation during the early pains of learning. To this day, I have never seen a better way of setting up a program for results — a true win-win.

**Don't Worry. This Offer Makes Money.** If you did give all the money back, this offer wouldn't make money — but it does. First, many won't qualify — even with realistic conditions. Second, those who do qualify often stay as customers. But they can only stay customers if they have something else to buy. So have an upsell ready to apply their winnings (Section III).

**Only Offer "Win Your Money Back" If You Feel Ok With Giving Money Back!** Refunds are a part of doing business. However, when advertised well, the Win Your Money Back offer gets tons of extra customers. And when you give satisfied customers a great follow-up offer, you make plenty of profit. This more than outweighs the refunds. From the data we've collected from thousands of gyms, about 10% of all customers will ask for their money back. If you can't stomach it, don't do this.

**Offer Store Credit Instead of Cash.** If you don't want to offer cash back, you can offer store credit instead. My testing showed offering store credit and cash back got the same number of customers. So you might as well offer store credit. But, if you still want to advertise it as "free," pair it with an unconditional satisfaction guarantee. Adding the unconditional guarantee never materially affected the number of people who wanted their money back. Check with legal counsel in your area.

**Don't Take Blood Money.** If someone doesn't want me to have their money, I want it less than they do. As a personal rule, if a customer asks for a refund — entitled or not — I give it to them. Just focus on getting the next customer.

**How To Create Your Win Your Money Back Criteria.** These criteria make or break this offer. Good criteria have three characteristics:

1. **Easy To Track.** Train them on exactly what they need to do (or they will mess up). Bonus points if people already do it. Ex: Phones already track steps. Word processors already track word count. Cameras automatically date photos.

2. **Gets Customers Results.** Make criteria likely to get them their desired results. Realistic criteria do just fine. If you think the criteria look too easy, you've probably gotten close to realistic. Ex: attend meetings, workouts, watch videos, etc. Whatever stuff the best customers do to get the best results, make everyone do it.

3. **Advertises The Business.** Make advertising the business part of your criteria. For example: posting about their participation, tagging in social media, referring, or leaving reviews and testimonials.

**How You Apply Store Credit [IMPORTANT].** When customers win their money back, offer to apply it over a longer period or to a bulk package. Just offer to apply it to something that costs more than their winnings. In my experience, this keeps customers engaged and makes you more money. Here's what that looks like:

- You have a product or service that costs $200 dollars per month.
- A customer wins $600 of credit. Avoid giving them three free months up front.
- Instead apply the $600 over 12 months: $600 / 12 months = $50/mo discount.
  - They now pay: $200 per month - $50 discount = **$150 per month**
- To be clear, they can use credit however they want. But, I recommend you present this first. If they ask to use it up front, you can share my experience — people fall off if they don't pay something. A discount over the long haul keeps them engaged over the long haul. So it's in the customer's best interest to keep some skin in the game.
- In-depth details on this Upsell Offer are in the Rollover Upsell chapter (Section III).

**All Meetings And Calls Provide Opportunities To Make More Offers.** Make check-in meetings part of your money-back criteria whenever you can. And make all meetings required to win their money back. Beyond helping them succeed, they are the best opportunities to make upsell offers. So after you've checked in, offer what makes sense based on their feedback. The Win Your Money Back Offer and my gyms had three appointments:

- **Nutrition Orientation** -> "Before pictures" -> I make a supplement offer.
- **Progress Check-in** -> I make a membership offer.
- **Transformation Feedback** -> "After pictures" -> I make the membership offer again.
  - If they bought the membership at the last meeting, I offered a discount if they prepaid for a year.

**Make Everyone A Winner.** Promote and sell the program as though they will only get it back if they meet the criteria. But, about halfway through, make your next offer as if they already won. You lower the customer's anxiety about failing and you'll keep them longer. They'll also love you that much more. Something like:

> "I know you're trying to hit this short-term goal, but what's your long-term goal?...okay that's great to hear. You get that it's not about this program but about your long-term results. Tell ya what, to show you how much I want you to hit that long-term goal, I'll credit this program toward the next one whether you hit the short-term goal or not — how's that sound?"

**At The End Of The Program, Let The "Losers" Win.** If someone refuses your first upsell and fails the challenge, you can still upsell them again. Here's how: act like they won. I say something like:

> "Don't worry about it. You started. That's the biggest victory of all. And even though you didn't hit your short-term goal, you met ours — which was finishing what you started. To show you that we're in this for the long haul, we'll credit your entire deposit towards staying with us long term. That way you get your money back and we can still hit your goal. How does that sound?"

You'll turn that frown upside down and they'll love you for it. Remember — **we don't get customers to make a sale, we make sales to get customers.**

#### Summary Points

Win Your Money Back is magical for businesses that require their customers to put in continuous efforts to get their ideal outcome.

- The Win Your Money Back Offer rocks because:
  - You get loads of up front cash.
  - You get more customers to say yes since you lower their risk.
  - You get massive results for customers.
  - You get more long-term customers.
  - They advertise your offer to get you even more customers.
- Making some meetings a part of the terms gives great opportunities to check in with your customers and make more offers specific to their needs.
- Everyone thinks businesses make money on people who fail the program. No. The real money comes from people who succeed with it and you have something else to offer them. Trust me on this one. The more results you deliver, the more money you'll make. Think long.
- Make refund criteria easy to track, aligned with customer goals, and helpful for the business.
- Only use a Win Your Money Back Offer if your refund rate is below 5%. Otherwise, fix your product before doing this.
- Put the store credit toward another, preferably more expensive, offer. You want them to stay customers...so give them the opportunity.
- To make more sales and keep more customers, make everyone a winner in private. That way, everyone stays surprised and grateful when you make your Upsell Offer.

### Giveaways

**Many will enter...many will win.**

**August 2020.**

I hopped on the phone with the owner of a fitness certification business to talk shop. In a few minutes, he gave me the rundown about how they certify fitness enthusiasts and help them find clients.

"Interesting business you've got," I said. "How do you get leads?"

"It's pretty simple. We advertise a full-ride scholarship to our entire program. People apply with their contact information and then answer a few questions. We ask stuff like 'Why should we pick you for the full-ride scholarship?' The best answer gets a full-ride. But we also do something more..."

"We give out partial scholarships."

And then it hit me.

"Ooohhh...So lots of people apply for the 'grand prize' and only one person gets that. But the other applicants qualify for smaller prizes?"

"Right. So I make a big deal out of the person who wins the full-ride scholarship, but then I call everyone else to let them know they got a partial scholarship. When I talk to them, they're thrilled. Most of them join our program on the spot."

***

He presented this as an education offer and as a fitness offer. But it's so much more. Free Giveaways generate many leads who show interest in your most expensive product. What could be better?

#### Description

Giveaway Offers advertise a chance to win a big prize in exchange for contact information and whatever else you want. Then, after picking a winner, you offer everyone else the big prize at a discounted price. Giveaways also go by names like "scholarship" and "sweepstakes" and "raffles" etc. They all mean "enter for a chance to win." To run a Giveaway Offer you:

- Pick a Grand Prize.
- Pick your promotional offer.
- Ask for contact information and other eligibility criteria.
- Pick what actions you want entrants to take to qualify for the big prize.
- Put the giveaway on a deadline to add urgency.
- Announce the Grand Prize winner and contact everyone else.

**Pick A Grand Prize.** Make your Grand Prize the thing you want everyone to buy. Make sure you assign a monetary value to your grand prize to serve as a price anchor. For instance, if you sell $5,000 worth of value for $2,000, then advertise the $5,000 value!

**Pick Your Promotional Offer.** Your promotional offer takes the place of the "partial scholarship" in the story. You create it by enhancing your core offer with a discount, a bonus, or by minorly changing it from the Grand Prize in order to ethically justify a price reduction (using the Grand Prize as a price anchor). And the bigger the discount, the more compelling the offer.

**Ask For Contact Information.** In exchange for a chance to win, ask for permission to contact them any way you please for follow-up promotions.

**Eligibility.** I ask if they're a fit for my products. Like "Do you own a vet clinic?" or more character/need-based questions like "Why should you be selected?"

**Qualifying Actions.** Other stuff entrants do to qualify to win. I also use these to get them to promote my giveaway more, or demonstrate higher levels of interest. Ex: attending a call or event, making a post, entering a group, etc.

**Put The Giveaway On A Deadline To Add Urgency.** Set a date for the Grand Prize drawing. I like three to seven days from the day I start promoting it. Update leads daily. First, let them know how long they have left. Second, provide value along with your countdown. Show everyone the benefits of the grand prize, how excited they should be, and refer everyone to social proof.

**Announce The Grand Prize Winner And Start Contacting Everyone Else.** Announce the Grand Prize winner publicly, then message everyone else who qualifies for your core offer privately. The beauty is — everybody else qualifies for your promotional offer. To make sure they redeem, add another deadline. Make claiming your promotional offer expire in seven days.

**Explain The Cost-To-Value Using Their Discount.** My rule of thumb: make your core offer discount equal to 10%--30% of your gross margins. Say we advertise a Grand Prize with a $5,000 value with a $2,000 retail price tag. Everyone else gets it for $1,800 (a 10% discount off retail). By comparing the value of the thing to what they pay, a 10% discount becomes a **64% difference in cost-to-value!**

#### Example Free Giveaways

**Dentist Offer — Free Perfect Smile Giveaway**

- Grand Prize: A free set of invisible braces — $6,000 retail price
- Promotional Offer: $2,000 gift card for braces

**Physical Products Offer — Free Year Of Organic Dog Food**

- Grand Prize: Free Year of Organic Dog Food — $1,000 retail price
- Promotional Offer: $300 gift card for dog food only useable with a one-year subscription

**Services Offer — Free Ultimate Giveaway**

- Grand Prize: Free 1-Year Package — $5,000 retail price
- Promotional Offer: $2,000 voucher redeemable toward 1-Year Service Agreement

**Consulting Offer — Free 16-Week Turnaround Giveaway**

- Grand Prize: 16-Week Turnaround — $12,000 retail price
- Promotional Offer: $6,000 Partial Scholarship

#### Important Notes

**Consult Legal Counsel About How To Structure Your Giveaway.**

**Eligibility Criteria Get More Customers To Buy Your Core Offer.** I ask questions like: Why should we pick you? Why this program? Why now? Why does this matter to you? What's your goal?

**If Your Giveaway Doesn't Work, It Means Your Grand Prize Wasn't Grand Enough.** One of my portfolio companies ran a giveaway with tickets to their event as the grand prize. Not compelling. They tried again with a $50,000 bundle of equipment and it crushed.

**Give Away Two Prizes For Twice The Leads.** Tell everyone that if someone they refer wins the grand prize, they win one too. That way, they get endless "entries" by referring friends.

**Scarcity, Scarcity, Scarcity.** Limit your giveaway by time, number of entries, or both. I like both. I match how many people I let enter with the number I can manage to call in seven days.

**Urgency, Urgency, Urgency.** I add urgency in three places — to enter, to claim, to use. Always have deadlines.

**Have Downsells Available.** At the start of the call, let them know they qualified for two prizes. Present your promotional offer first. If not, offer the same discount on any other product.

**If You Have A Recurring Revenue Business.** Apply their discount over the longest period of time they'll agree to. Then, set up their monthly subscription to bill automatically at normal rates after the discounted period ends.

#### Summary Points

- At their core, Giveaways ask an audience to apply to get a high-value thing of yours for free. Many will enter — one wins. The rest qualify for discounts on your core offer.
- Pick a Grand Prize people want.
- Give two prizes away if you want more people to refer.
- You can get great information from every lead because you can make it part of the entry process.
- Advertise your Giveaway for seven days, or until the number of leads surpasses the number of people you can manage to call in seven days, whichever comes first.
- Book appointments with everyone else to claim your promotional offer.
- Putting an expiration date on people claiming their prize makes them more likely to claim it.
- If somebody says no to your core offer, have another product or service to discount.

### Decoy Offer

**Which one do you think will get you the best results?**

**June 2014.**

John, another early mentor, retired early. He spent his retirement raising his girls, playing golf, and hanging out at his lake house. On those long drives, he taught me things about life and business that I use to this day. His lesson: **The 5-Day $5 VIP Tanning Pass.**

"The beauty of the 5-Day Pass is everyone thinks they can get tan in five days. And they can. But, it's never as tan as they want to get. So, when someone comes in with a pass, we ask them how tan they wanna get. Then we give them 'the turkey talk' — a Thanksgiving turkey takes three hours to cook. We all know what happens if you double the temperature. You burn it! It takes at least five to ten sessions to get the color they want without burning."

"'Let's just credit your VIP pass toward your first month. Why buy so many $25 day passes when members get unlimited access for just $19.99?'"

**Five years later...**

Fitness leads had gotten way too expensive. I remembered the $5 VIP Tanning pass and we created a new offer: two options. The first option is free — one session per week. The second option is an "Ultimate" Version for $399 with unlimited sessions, 1-1 coaching, personalized stuff, and a guarantee. Eight out of ten people took the $399 option.

***

John's philosophy: **giving customers what they want now, so you can give them what they need later.** Making our premium the clear solution is what Decoy Offers are all about.

#### Description

Decoy Offers advertise something free or discounted. Then, when leads ask to learn more, you also present a more valuable premium offer. The premium offer provides more features, benefits, bonuses, guarantees, and so on. By putting your decoy offers and premium offers side-by-side, leads can see how much more valuable your premium offer is. Either way, you can close everyone. This makes it cheap and profitable to get new customers.

Steps:

1. Advertise a lesser, smaller, or simpler version of your premium offer as a decoy.
2. When leads engage, offer both options, but emphasize the premium one.

#### Examples

**Lemonade Stand Offer (Physical Products)**

- Attraction Offer: "Free Week of Lemonade" OR "$1 Week of Lemonade."
- Decoy Option: Water + powdered lemon + corn syrup
- Premium Option: Organic, all-natural, imported Italian lemons, cold-processed and shipped to your door. Comes with other flavors.

**Float Tank Center (Service)**

- Attraction Offer: "Free 6-Week Stress Release" OR "$6 6-Week Stress Release."
- Decoy Option: One float per month with at-home DIY stress relief exercises.
- Premium Option: Two times per week floats for 6 weeks, 1-1 consulting, journal, sleep routine. Satisfaction guaranteed.

**Gym Offer (Local Business)**

- Attraction Offer: "Free 21-Day Transformation" OR "$21 21-Day Transformation."
- Decoy Option: Workouts done in a group once a day. General nutrition plan. No support. No guarantee.
- Premium Option: Unlimited workouts, personalized nutrition plan, 1-1 accountability, results guaranteed (or you get another 21 days free).

#### Important Notes

**How To Make Your Decoy Offer.** Offer fewer components, older models, or fewer personalized versions of your premium offer. Also, remove any guarantees.

**Advertise Benefits Not The Features.** We advertise a transformation in 21 days, not workouts and meal plans.

**You Can Advertise Discounts in Four Ways:**

1. **Percentage Off:** 25% off
2. **Absolute Amount:** $300 off
3. **Free Portion:** 3 Months Free
4. **The Total Package:** One Year For $900 ($1,200)

**Make The Contrast Huge.** The bigger the contrast, the better the deal, and the more customers will take it.

**Discount Offers Have Higher Show-Up Rates Than Free Offers.** If you have low show-up rates for appointments, try a Discount Offer instead.

**Get Them To Give You Permission To Sell Them.** Ask: **"Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"** As soon as they say "results," skip to your premium offer.

**When Making Your Premium Offer, Get Excited About It.** Use an "assumed close." No hype. Just friendly disposition. Almost bored over how regularly people buy.

**Surprise Benefit (Optional).** If someone takes the decoy option, surprise them with a few low-/zero-cost features from your premium offer to build goodwill.

#### Summary Points

- Decoy Offers advertise something free or discounted. Then present a more valuable premium offer.
- Make the premium option far more valuable by adding more features, benefits, bonuses, and guarantees.
- Strip down your Decoy Offer as much as reasonable.
- Ask "are you here for free stuff or lasting results?" for permission to offer the premium thing first.
- You can still make money from leads who pick the decoy option.
- Expect to make money fast. If you're not, make the contrast between offers larger.

### Buy X Get Y Free

**Downtown Nashville 2020.**

In Buy X Get Y Free Offers, when customers buy something, they get other stuff free. The more free stuff they get, and the higher its value, the better it works. By selling more than one thing at once, you can turn Discount Offers into even stronger Free Offers.

Example: I could sell three t-shirts for $10 each for a total of $30 or I could sell one t-shirt for $30 and give two away free. It's the same price, but way more free stuff!

#### Summary Points

- In Buy X Get Y Free Offers, when customers buy something, they get other stuff free.
- Always try to give more free things than paid things.
- You can pair different free things with your paid things.
- Buy X Get Y Free can lengthen customer retention. More time = more upsell opportunities.

### Pay Less Now or Pay More Later

> "Time is money." — Benjamin Franklin

**June 2016.**

In Pay Less Now or Pay More Later, you give people a choice to pay full-price later OR pay a discounted price now. This removes all risk from the customer. They pay later and only if they like it. Combining delayed payment and satisfaction guarantee. Once they agree to pay later, you can get them to pay now with hefty discounts and valuable bonuses.

The pay later option allows you to advertise "free" and gets their card on file. Pay now options provide a **20-50% discount** and greater bonuses.

#### Examples

**Find Your First Real Estate Deal — Free 3-Day Workshop**

#### Important Notes

**Promise A Clear Yes/No Result.** Keep the promise simple, clear, and measurable.

**Make a Conditional Satisfaction Guarantee.** People can only cancel if they qualify (e.g., attended the training).

**Optimizing:** If too many people take "pay later," discount "pay now" more or add better bonuses. If too many take "pay now," do the opposite.

**This Works For Recurring Revenue Businesses Too.** Pay a higher ongoing rate 30 days later or pay less today and keep the lower rate for good.

#### Summary Points

- Pay Less Now Or Pay More Later Offers give people a choice to pay full-price later OR pay a discounted price with bonuses if they pay now.
- The Pay Later option has a delayed payment with a conditional guarantee.
- The Pay Now option offers a 20-50% discount and bonuses.
- Make your promise easy to track, difficult to refute, and a clear yes/no result.
- If more than 10% cancel, adjust your promise, guarantee conditions, or price.

## Section III: Upsell Offers

> "Do you want fries with that?" — McDonald's Famous Upsell

With an Attraction Offer in place, you've got customers and cash. Now we want to maximize 30-day profits. Answer: make more money by making Upsell Offers. Upsells just mean **whatever we offer next.**

### How Upsells Work

When an offer solves a problem, another appears. You upsell the solution to the problem your offer reveals. Often, upsells make the majority of the profit.

**Burger shop example:** $0.25 profit on a $2.00 burger. Add fries (+$0.75 profit), a drink (+$1.75 profit), supersize (+$1.00 profit). Profit goes from $0.25 to $3.00 — a **11.6x increase**. If McDonald's didn't upsell fries and soda, there wouldn't be a McDonald's.

**Upsells fail when:**

- You offer something they don't want.
- You offer it at the wrong time.
- You offer it the wrong way.

**Upsells tend to offer:**

- **More of what they just got** (think quantity)
- **Better versions of it** (think quality)
- **New or complementary stuff** (think different)

**Four Upsell Offers:**

1. The Classic Upsell
2. Menu Upsells
3. Anchor Upsells
4. Rollover Upsells

---

### The Classic Upsell

**You Can't Have X Without Y!**

**Summer 2016.** A fourth-generation fur coat dealer's son came up with something genius: advertise free earmuffs with coat storage. When customers come, say "Great. And we'll store those as well for $30. You don't want to store anything else do you?" They say no — and buy.

#### Description

The Classic Upsell offers a solution to the customer's next problem the moment they become aware of it. **Your core offer solves one problem and creates another. Your upsell immediately solves that next problem.** Like the rental car: You can't have a car without insurance. You can't have a car without gas.

**Bottom Line:** If a problem appears, and you can solve it immediately — in exchange for money — do it!

#### Examples

- **Car Wash** -> Upsell: Sealant
- **Bicycle** -> Upsell #1: Helmet, #2: Lights, #3: Puncture-Resistant Tires
- **Course on exercising** -> Upsell: Nutrition course

#### Important Notes

**Actually Do It.** Many businesses only sell one thing. I tell them "You barely have a business — you have a front end. Figure out what you're gonna offer next."

**Offer More Profitable Upsells First.**

**Get Them To "Say No To Say Yes."** "You don't want anything else do you?" turns a "no" into "yes."

**Surprise And Delight.** If they say yes before you add all bonuses, still give them all.

**Sell More When They're Buying More — Hyper Buying Cycle.** Short window when people are most excited about a new thing. Think weddings, new hobbies, babies, moving.

**Use Free Bonuses To Create Problems Upsell Offers Solve.**

**The Faster People Get Access To Stuff, The More They'll Value It.**

**If You Bundle Upsells, Name Them.** "Fastest results" bundle, "Transformation package," "Minimum package."

**Make Sure You Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting (BAMFAM).** End every appointment by scheduling the next appointment.

**Upsell Guarantees, Warranties, and Insurance.** Add 5-50% onto the price in exchange for a guarantee.

#### Summary

- Your Attraction Offer reveals a problem. Upsells solve it.
- "You don't want anything else do you?" gets people to agree by saying no.
- Give access as soon as possible.
- Give away bonuses that create upsell opportunities.
- BAMFAM as a way of life.
- **The worst thing is if they would've said yes but you never asked.**
- You can charge for guarantees, warranties, or insurance rather than give them away free.

### Menu Upsell

**You don't need that...you need this**

Through three stories from supplement selling, I discovered four tactics: **the A/B Upsell** (ask which product they prefer, not whether they want it), **Card On File** (ask to use the card already on file), **Prescription Upselling** (detailed personalized instructions upsell more than vague suggestions), and **Unselling** (cross out what they don't need to build goodwill for what they do).

#### Description

In a Menu Upsell, you tell customers which options they don't need. Then, tell them what they do need, their preferences, and how to get their value from it. Menu Upsells combine up to four tactics:

1. **Unselling** — Tell customers what they don't need so you can emphasize what they do.
2. **Prescription Upsell** — Tell them what they do need. Explain how it integrates with what they already bought. Personalize and detail how to maximize its value.
3. **A/B Upsell** — Ask their preference between A and B. Either choice results in an upsell. Remove the option of not buying.
4. **Card On File** — "Do you want to use the card on file?" Lowers hidden costs of buying.

#### Examples

**Massage Therapist:** Unsell lymphatic massage (not needed) -> Prescribe trigger points + stretches -> A/B: before work or on the way home? -> Card on file.

**Dog Food:** Unsell small bag and puppy stuff -> Prescribe joint chews and heartworm wafers -> A/B: beef or chicken flavor? -> Card on file.

**Digital Product:** Unsell 7 of 8 courses -> Prescribe the one they need -> A/B: DM or phone support? -> Card on file.

#### Important Notes

**Make Anything A/B Sellable:** Quantity, start dates, flavors, time slots, sizes, colors, personnel, delivery speeds, etc.

**Add A Nudge:** "This is my favorite" or "X is usually a safe bet."

**Employees Love Unselling.** Encourage them to help customers "game the system."

#### Summary Points

- Menu Upsells combine Unselling, Prescribing, A/B Offer, and Card On File.
- Unselling lower-margin stuff incentivizes higher-margin upsells.
- Nudge new customers towards what makes sense for them.

---

### Anchor Upsell

**The only thing worse than making a $1,000 offer to a person with a $100 budget... is making a $100 offer to someone with a $1,000 budget.**

**2016.** Budgeted $500 for a suit. First suit shown: $16,000. Gasp. Owner asked "Do you care about the designer much?" Next suit: $2,200. Bought it plus $300 in accessories. **Spent five times more than budgeted and felt OK about it.**

#### Description

With Anchor Upsells, you offer premium stuff first. If the customer gasps, you offer a cheaper but acceptable alternative. Present a premium version **5x-10x the price** first. Then your main offer looks like a much better deal.

**Steps:**

1. Present the Anchor — the really expensive thing.
2. Get "The Gasp."
3. Come to the rescue — ask if they care about what makes it premium.
4. Present your main offer.
5. Ask how they wanna pay.

**Bonuses:** Anchored customers spend more than they normally would. Some customers still buy the expensive thing.

#### Examples

- **Lawn Care:** Premium $1,000/week (cell phone number, fancy mulch) vs. Main $200/week
- **Painting:** Premium $1,000 (20-year insurance, gift wrapped) vs. Main $200
- **Newsletter:** Premium $199/mo (all previous + new + early) vs. Main $19/mo

#### Important Notes

**If You Treat The Anchor Like A Fake, So Will The Customer.** Actually sell it.

**Make A Premium Offer You Actually Want People To Buy.** Some customers will.

**The bigger the gasp, the more they bought.**

**To Get More People To Buy Your Main Offer:** Keep the same primary features. Change secondary "premium" features. Offering the primary features for a fifth of the price makes the main offer a great deal.

#### Summary

- Present anchor. Get gasp. Come to the rescue. Present core offer. Ask for payment.
- Make your premium offer 5-10x more expensive.
- Anchored customers spend more than they plan to.
- Some customers will buy the premium offer. Outsized profits with fewer sales.
- Same primary features. Different secondary features.

---

### Rollover Upsell

**Wanna just roll it forward?**

**June 2014.** Running a Win Your Money Back Offer at my gym. $600 fitness programs. Problem: winners put their $600 toward three months of membership then churned. Friend Justin solved it: "I just roll over their winnings into a year long membership. We just give them fifty bucks off per month for a year." So they start paying immediately.

#### Description

Rollover Upsells credit some or all of a customer's previous purchases toward your next offer.

**Who to upsell:** Re-engage old customers, rescue upset customers, "rescue" other people's upset customers, upsell regular customers.

**What to upsell:** More, better, or different. Roll credit to something more expensive.

**How:** Apply all or part of the discount up front or spread it over time.

#### Examples

- **Chiropractor Winback:** Reach out to old patients. Offer $500 credit toward long-term plan.
- **Dentist:** Credit $200 cleaning toward teeth whitening package.
- **Software:** Credit competitors' customers' remaining payments to switch to yours.
- **Membership:** Spread $600 first purchase as $50/month discount for 12 months.

#### Important Notes

**Use Rollover Offers To Attract New Customers.** Find leads from negative product reviews. Rollover Upsell all of them.

**Do Rollover Upsells before refunding.** Saved me tons of customers and cash.

**Winback campaigns:** Personalized videos for 200 past customers offering $4,000 credit. 20% took the offer. **~$1,900,000 of annual revenue from one day of recording.**

**Add Urgency. Make Them One-Time Only.** They take it now or pay full price later.

**Pricing:** Make the upsell offer **at least 4x more** than the rollover credit. 25% max discount.

#### Summary Points

- Rollover Upsells credit previous purchases toward your next offer.
- Who: old, upset, competitors', current customers.
- What: more, better, different. Make a profit after credit.
- How: Full or partial. Up front or spread out.
- Price next offer at least 4x higher than the credit.
- Add urgency. Make it one-time only.

---

### Upsell Offers Conclusion

**Solve rich people problems, they pay better.**

Four most powerful Upsells: **The Classic Upsell, Menu Upsells, Anchor Upsells, and Rollover Upsells.** Many businesses go from burning cash to printing it — overnight.

## Section IV: Downsell Offers

**What to offer when they say no.**

**Downselling tweaks the original offer to find the highest value solution for the customer's budget.** I downsell by changing **how they pay** or **what they get**.

### The Rules of Downselling

- **They Said No To This Offer, Not All Offers.** No means no for this thing, not everything.
- **Downsells Are Trades.** If you give something, get something.
- **Personalize, Don't Pressure.** Offer more of what they like, less of what they don't.
- **Offer The Same Things In New Ways.** A hundred ways to offer the stuff you already have.
- **Don't Drop Your Price Just To Get Somebody To Buy.** That's discounting, not downselling.
- **Customers Talk About Price.** Don't charge somebody less in the moment just because you're scared.

**Three Downsell Processes:**

1. Payment Plan Downsells (how they pay)
2. Trial With Penalty (how they pay)
3. Feature Downsells (what they get)

---

### Payment Plan Downsells

**How much can you put down today?**

"I can't afford it." In my desperation I blurted out "OK, when do you get paid?" My first ever payment plan.

Payment plans make money one way but lose it two ways. They make more money when more customers complete payments. They lose money when people cancel early. They lose the most when people who would have paid in full take a payment plan and cancel.

#### The 7-Step Payment Plan Downsell Process

**Step 1) Reward For Paying In Full.** Present price with interest included. Offer prepayment as a discount. "It's $15...but it's $10 if you prepay it. You save $5...that's what most people do."

**Step 2) 3rd-Party Financing, Credit Card, Layaway.**

**Step 3) Half Now, Half Later.** Schedule off paychecks (every two weeks).

**Step 4) Check: Do They Still Want It?** "On a scale from 1-10 how bad do you wanna do this?" 8+ = keep offering. 7 or below = sell something different.

**Step 5) Split Into Three Payments.**

**Step 6) Evenly Spread Payments.**

**Step 7) Offer A Free Trial.** (Next chapter.)

#### Important Notes

**"Seesaw" Downselling:** "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" Frame payment plan as negative, prepaying as positive.

**Payment Plans Have Built-In Upsells:** Offer the prepay discount during their payment plan.

**Align payment schedules with paycheck schedules.** Run declined payments a few times that day. Recoups a third of declines.

**Billing Cadence Affects Monthly Churn** (Profitwell data, 14,000 businesses):

- Monthly: **10.7%** monthly cancellation
- Quarterly: **5%** monthly cancellation
- Annual: **2%** monthly cancellation

Start high, work down.

#### Summary Points

- Payment Plan Downsells spread cost into scheduled payments.
- They get more buyers like discounts but maintain full price over time.
- Step 1: Discount for pay-in-full. Step 2: 3rd-party options. Step 3: Split in two. Step 4: Temperature check. Step 5: Split in three. Step 6: Spread evenly. Step 7: Free trial.
- Align payments with paychecks.

---

### Trial With Penalty

**If you do X, Y, Z, I'll let you start for free.**

Leila's HR software: "If I did their training I'd get free onboarding. But if I skipped the training I'd have to pay for it!" Result: she learned the software, now she doesn't want to switch.

#### Description

In a Trial With Penalty offer, customers try your product for free so long as they meet your terms. **Customers only pay if they don't meet the terms.** The terms should be things that make excellent customers.

It's not "see if you like it." It's "you get it for free so long as you do this stuff...which makes you a perfect fit for my next offer."

#### Examples

**B2C: 28 Day Blueprint** — Attend calls, post progress weekly, journal daily, attend sessions.

**B2B: 5-Day Challenge** — Send 100 outbound messages/day, attend daily training, attend graduation call.

**Software: $500 Onboarding** — Attend three Zoom calls, do homework, activate profile, get employees set up.

#### How To Downsell The Trial (5 Steps)

1. **Offer The Trial Last.** "How about we just get ya started for free?"
2. **Always Get A Credit Card.** "That's just how we've always done it."
3. **Always Sell Staying And Paying.** "If this got you the result, will you stay long-term?"
4. **Explain fees after getting the card.**
5. **Make Check-ins Required** (upsell opportunities).

#### How I Upsell From A Trial

1. **If they like it:** Offer longer term or higher value version.
2. **If they hate it:** Ask what they'd change. Get angry at yourself. Offer higher-level thing. About half buy.
3. **If they didn't use it:** Offer to waive fee if they meet with you.

#### Summary Points

- Customers try free so long as they meet terms. Pay if they don't.
- Get the card, get the commitment, explain terms, explain consequences.
- Penalties get more paying customers than normal free trials.
- Use same criteria as Win Your Money Back.
- Break fees up by criteria rather than one lump fee.
- Use mid-trial check-ins to make more offers.

---

### Feature Downsells

**Why don't we try this instead?**

"I lower the price, but I justify it by cutting a feature. That way, I'm not discounting." He cut the money-back guarantee. Result: 25% close rate became 75% (35 buy the main thing, 40 take the downsell).

#### Description

Feature Downsells lower prices by changing what customers get. **People see the value in what you removed after they see the difference in price.** Remove features from highest to lowest value.

**Formula:** Take something away, lower the price, ask "how about now?"

#### Feature Downsell Types

- **Quantity:** 3-month supply -> 1-month supply. 4 sessions/month -> 2.
- **Quality:** Leather seats -> vinyl. 5-minute response -> overnight.
- **Service Quality Features:** Time availability, location, cancellation policy, speed of response, speed of delivery, service ratio, communication method, provider qualifications, live vs. recorded, in-person vs. remote, DIY/DWY/DFY, expirations, personalization, insurance/guarantee.
- **Removing Entire Features:** Drop calls, keep chat and email.
- **Done-For-You to Do-It-Yourself:** Chiropractor adjustments -> at-home tools.

#### Important Notes

**Never Negotiate The Price.** If they want to pay less now — payment plan. Less overall — feature downsell. But never less "just because."

**Maintain The Position Of A Helpful Guide.** Collaborative, not competitive.

**Name Your Feature Combinations.** "The Whale Package," First Class -> Business Class -> Economy.

**Name The Cheapest "The Minimum."** "So nothing more than the minimum package then?"

**Temperature Check After Two Downsells.** 1-10 scale. 8+ = payment plan downselling. Below 7 = recombine features. You can alternate between payment plan and feature downsells.

**After Each Downsell, Ask "Deal?" Or "Fair Enough?"**

**Feature Downsell Your Guarantees.** Removing the guarantee often flips a "no" back to a "yes."

**Feature Downsell Current Customers.** Customers downsold into a lower package just for them have the **second highest value** of all customers.

**Barter With Reviews, Testimonials, And Referrals.** "$100 off if you: leave reviews, video testimonial, social posts, introduce two friends."

#### Summary Points

- Take something away, lower the price, ask "how about now?"
- If you remove stuff they hate and lower price a lot -> more take downsell.
- If you remove stuff they love and lower price a little -> more take original offer.
- First downsell: reconsider original. Rest: find best deal for them.
- Feature downsell current customers before they cancel.
- Discount in exchange for advertising.

---

### Downsell Offers Conclusion

**Everybody buys something.**

Downsells: a hundred different offers for the same product. The offer is never the same stuff for cheaper. We keep tweaking until it's the best deal for them.

## Section V: Continuity Offers

> "You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once." — John, an early mentor

Continuity Offers provide ongoing value that customers make ongoing payments for — until they cancel. Sell once, get paid again and again.

**Example:** $1,000 thing to 100 people: 10 buy = $10,000. Same thing at $50/month: 40 buy. After 20 months: **$40,000 total.** Plus 4x the customers to upsell.

By making continuity offers last, you get cash today from Attraction/Upsell/Downsell and cash tomorrow from Continuity.

### The Three Continuity Offers

1. Continuity: Bonus Offers
2. Continuity: Discount Offers
3. Waived Fee Offer

---

### Continuity Bonus Offers

**If you like this, you're gonna love what I have next...**

A gym owner's hack: "If they become a member, we'll make the challenge free. Plus they get member-exclusive bonuses." Result: Went from 17 continuity members to 40, plus 8 prepaid upsells.

#### Description

With Continuity Bonuses you give the customer an awesome thing if they sign up today. Typically, the bonus itself has more value than the first continuity payment.

- **Bonus** — adding value (products, programs, onboarding, setup).
- **Discount** — lowering costs. Do both to get the benefits of both.

#### Examples

- **Pet Food:** Get every dog toy we've ever made free ($800 value) when you sign up for $59/month food shipments.
- **Service:** $1,000 Accelerator free when you become a member for $100/month.
- **Digital:** Get all 40 past newsletters ($15,880 value) by becoming a member for $399/mo.

#### Important Notes

**Focus On The Bonus, Not The Membership.** Advertise the free valuable thing, not "join my membership."

**Keep Bonuses Related To Your Core Offer.**

**Make Bonuses Things You Already Have And Do.** Past newsletters, onboarding you do anyway.

**You Can Bonus Customers By Giving Them Titles.** Silver, gold, diamond, etc. Some customers care more about the title than any other bonus.

**Pricing For Continuity vs. Up Front Cash:**

- 50% choose continuity: standalone at **1.33x** more
- 60% choose continuity: standalone at **1.66x** more
- 70% choose continuity: standalone at **2x** more
- 80% choose continuity: standalone at **2.33x** more
- 90% choose continuity: standalone at **2.66x** more

**Bulk Prepaid Discounts:** "Buy five months get one free." Only 1 in 8 needs to take it to raise 30-day profits by 50%.

#### Summary Points

- Bonuses get more people to start continuity.
- Advertise what you give away, not what you sell.
- More bonuses + more discounts = more sign-ups.
- Sell the value of the bonus before telling them how to get it free.
- Price standalone at 33% above continuity for 50/50 split.
- Boost cash with bulk prepaid discounts.

---

### Continuity Discount Offers

**If you sign up today, you get X time free.**

The trash man: "I'd do their trash for a whole year free if they contracted me to do the next five years paid." After the first year, cash came flooding in. He sold the whole thing for a pretty penny.

#### Description

Give products or services away for free if the customer commits to buying more over time.

**Four Discount Methods:**

1. **Up Front.** Apply discount up front, push out the term. Delays cash.
2. **At The End.** Apply at the end. They earn free time.
3. **Spread Over Time.** 3 months free on a 12-month commitment = $50/month discount.
4. **After first 1-2 payments.** "First and last month" or activation fee.

#### Important Notes

**HIGHEST VALUE NOTE IN THIS BOOK: Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly.** 12 months vs. 13 four-week cycles = 8.3% more annually. **If your business has 20% margins, this adds 41% to annual profit.** Just change a few words. Has made millions in pure profit.

**Don't Eat Into The Term, Extend It.** 12 months paid + 3 months free = 15 total months.

**Get 3% More Revenue:** "It's $X plus a 3% processing fee." **If you run a 10% profit business, adding 3% adds 30% to profit.**

**Get Two Forms Of Payment.** Offer to save the processing fee in exchange for a second form of payment.

**Get ACH If You Can.** Cheapest way to transact besides cash.

**Gift Cards.** Physical cards they can use or gift to a friend. Many forget to use them = full-priced sign-up.

**Lifetime Discount At Your Most Common Churn Point.** Lower rate if they stay past the month your average customer drops off.

**CANCELLATIONS:** Make the cancellation fee equal to the discount they received. Make sure customers know how to cancel. Ask for exit interviews — save a third of customers this way.

---

### Waived Fee Offer

**You can sign up month-to-month with a setup fee, or I'll waive it if you commit to a year.**

#### Description

Ask for a startup fee (**3-5x monthly rate**) as part of month-to-month. Waive the entire fee if they commit longer term. If they cancel early, they pay the fee.

**Bottom Line: Customers stay longer if leaving costs more than staying.**

#### Example

- Commitment: 12 months. Rate: $1,000/month. Fee: $5,000.
- **Option A:** Pay $5,000 fee + $1,000/month. Cancel whenever.
- **Option B:** Waive $5,000 if you commit to 12 months. Only pay fee if you break commitment.

#### Important Notes

**Fees Get Them To Start and Stick.** If cost to quit exceeds cost to stay, they stay.

**If More Than 5% Want To Cancel Early,** the product needs improvement.

**Smaller Fee = More Up Front Cash.** Larger Fee = More Commitments.

**Drop The Fee After Commitment Is Fulfilled.**

**Works best for commitments of one year and longer.**

**Cancellation Fees For A Cause:** "What cause do you hate? If you cancel early, I donate your fee to them."

---

### Continuity Offers Conclusion

**The only thing better than getting someone to buy once, is getting them to buy again.**

I make Continuity Offers last. Start with profitable Attraction Offers, then Upsell and Downsell, then Continuity. If they accept, upsell a bulk amount at a discount. Then they automatically enter continuity after the bulk purchase.
